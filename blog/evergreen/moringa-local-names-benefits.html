Moringa Around the World: Local Names & Benefits

Explore the various names of Moringa and how cultures worldwide incorporate this nutritious superfood into their diets.

Moringa Oleifera, also known as the "Miracle Tree," is used across the world for its incredible nutritional and medicinal benefits. Different regions have unique names for it, reflecting its global significance.

Local Names for Moringa

Depending on the country, Moringa goes by different names. Here are some of the most commonly used ones:

  • Philippines: Malunggay, Kamunggay
  • Indonesia & Malaysia: Daun Kelor
  • India: Sahjan (Hindi), Murungai (Tamil), Munagakaya (Telugu)
  • Nigeria: Zogale
  • Haiti: Benzolive

Health Benefits of Moringa

  • Rich in vitamins and minerals (Vitamin A, C, Iron)
  • Boosts immunity and supports overall health
  • Helps regulate blood sugar and cholesterol levels
  • Supports digestive health with high fiber content

How Moringa is Used in Different Cuisines

Different cultures incorporate Moringa into their diets in unique ways:

  • India: Used in curries like sambar and stir-fries.
  • Philippines: Common in Chicken Tinola and soups.
  • Nigeria: Moringa leaves are eaten raw in salads or mixed with peanut paste.
