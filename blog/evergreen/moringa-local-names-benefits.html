Moringa: Global Names, Health Benefits & Cultural Uses

Explore the diverse names of Moringa worldwide and uncover its remarkable health benefits and cultural significance.

Moringa Oleifera, often called the "Miracle Tree," is one of the most nutrient-dense plants in the world. Packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, it has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and local cuisines across Africa, Asia, and Latin America.

Moringa Names by Country & Region

Moringa in the Philippines & Indonesia In Southeast Asia, Moringa is widely consumed as a nutritious vegetable.

  • Philippines: Malunggay, Kamunggay (used in the Visayas and Mindanao regions)
  • Indonesia & Malaysia: Daun Kelor (widely used in herbal medicine and local dishes)

Moringa in India & South Asia Moringa is deeply rooted in Indian Ayurveda, with over 300 medicinal uses. It’s a common ingredient in curries, dals, and soups.

  • Hindi: Sahjan (सहजन)
  • Tamil: Murungai (முருங்கை)
  • Telugu: Munagakaya (మునగకాయ)
  • Kannada: Nugge Soppu (ನಗ್ಗೆ ಸೊಪ್ಪು)
  • Marathi: Shevga (शेवगा)
  • Bengali: Sanjina (সাঁজন)
  • Odia: Sajna (ସଜନ)

In Bangladesh, it’s known as Sajna Data, and in Sri Lanka, it’s called Murunga.

Moringa in Africa Moringa is an essential part of African traditional medicine and nutrition, especially in arid regions where food scarcity is common.

  • Nigeria & West Africa: Zogale (used in soups and salads)
  • Ghana: Yevu-Yevu
  • Senegal: Nébédaye (meaning "never die" because of its resilience)
  • Ethiopia: Shiferaw

Moringa in Latin America & the Caribbean In Latin America and the Caribbean, Moringa is known for its energy-boosting properties.

  • Haiti: Benzolive
  • Mexico & Central America: Árbol de Libertad ("Tree of Liberty")

Moringa in Europe & the Middle East Although less common in Europe, Moringa is gaining popularity as a superfood.

  • Spain: Marango
  • Portugal: Moringueiro
  • Arabic-speaking countries: Rawag

Health Benefits of Moringa

Moringa is known for its impressive nutritional profile:

  • Rich in Vitamins & Minerals – High in vitamins A, C, and E, plus calcium, iron, and potassium.
  • Boosts Immunity – Supports immune function and helps fight infections.
  • Supports Heart Health – May lower cholesterol and blood pressure.
  • Good for Digestion – High in fiber, which aids digestion and gut health.
  • Regulates Blood Sugar – Beneficial for diabetes management.

How Different Cultures Use Moringa

India: Moringa pods (drumsticks) are added to sambar (a South Indian lentil dish). The leaves are used in stir-fries, chutneys, and teas. Ayurveda prescribes Moringa for detoxification and energy boosting.

Philippines: Malunggay leaves are commonly added to chicken tinola, a traditional soup. Used in omelets, smoothies, and bread for added nutrition.

Indonesia & Malaysia: Daun Kelor is a key ingredient in traditional herbal soups and teas. Used in Jamu, an Indonesian herbal drink.

Africa: In Nigeria, Zogale leaves are eaten as salads or mixed with groundnut paste. In Senegal, Moringa is part of daily nutrition programs for children.

Latin America: Moringa tea is popular for weight loss and energy boosting. Leaves are used in soups and stews.

Why Knowing Local Names Matters for Moringa Lovers

Using local names for Moringa can:

  • ✅ Help people find it in local markets
  • ✅ Improve research and awareness
  • ✅ Connect with traditional knowledge about Moringa’s uses

If you’re searching for Moringa in different countries, knowing the local name makes it much easier!

FAQs About Moringa Around the World

  • Can I use Moringa leaves in all types of cooking? Yes! You can add Moringa leaves to soups, smoothies, teas, and curries. They can also be dried and powdered for easy use.
  • Where can I buy Moringa in different countries? In the Philippines, Malunggay is available in local markets and grocery stores. In India, fresh drumstick leaves are sold in vegetable markets. In Indonesia, Daun Kelor is available in herbal stores and traditional markets. In Africa and Latin America, Moringa is sold in health stores and online.

Moringa is a global superfood with different names across cultures. Whether you call it Malunggay, Sahjan, Daun Kelor, or Drumstick Tree, its health benefits remain the same.
